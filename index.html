Privacy Policy

The Paranormal Archivist · Android application

Effective 21 July 2026 · Version 1.0

The short version: The Paranormal Archivist collects nothing about you. It has no user accounts, no analytics, no advertising, no crash reporting, and no third-party trackers of any kind.

The app has no internet access at all — the released version does not request the Android INTERNET permission, so it is technically incapable of transmitting your information anywhere. Every recording, reading, and note you create stays on your device until you delete it.

1. Who this policy covers

This policy applies to The Paranormal Archivist Android application ("the app"). It describes how the app handles information — which, as described below, means how it deliberately avoids handling it.

2. Information we collect

None. We do not collect, receive, transmit, store, sell, or share any personal information. We operate no servers, and the app is not capable of contacting any.

Specifically, the app does not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any account identifiers — there are no accounts
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or fingerprinting data
  • Location data — the app does not request location permission
  • Usage statistics, event logs, or analytics of any kind
  • Crash reports or diagnostic telemetry
  • Contacts, photos, calendar entries, or other apps' data

3. Information stored on your device

The app creates and stores data locally on your device only, in the app's private storage area. Other apps cannot read it. This includes:

  • Investigation logs — timestamps, location names you type in yourself, and your written session notes
  • Sensor readings — magnetic field measurements recorded during a session
  • Audio recordings — ambient audio clips you choose to record and save
  • App settings — such as your selected visual theme

This information never leaves your device. It is not backed up to us, because there is no "us" to back it up to.

4. Permissions the app requests, and why

PermissionWhy it is needed
Microphone
RECORD_AUDIO		 To record ambient audio clips when you press record. Recording only happens when you start it. Audio is written to your device's private storage and is never transmitted. If you decline this permission, the rest of the app continues to work.

The app also reads your device's magnetometer (magnetic field sensor) to operate its EMF meter. Android does not classify motion and environmental sensors as a permission-gated resource, and these readings are used only to draw the on-screen instrument and to save readings you explicitly choose to log.

The app does not request internet access, location, camera, contacts, storage outside its own private directory, or any other permission.

5. Third parties

The app contains no third-party SDKs — no advertising networks, no analytics providers, no crash-reporting services, no social media integrations. Nothing in the app profiles you or shares data with anyone, because nothing in the app communicates externally.

Fonts used in the app are bundled into the application package rather than downloaded at runtime, specifically so that opening the app does not generate any network request.

6. Your control over your data

Because everything is stored locally, you have complete control:

  • Delete an individual investigation log from within the app — its saved audio clips are deleted from storage along with it
  • Clear all app data through Android's Settings › Apps › The Paranormal Archivist › Storage
  • Uninstall the app, which removes all of its data from your device

We cannot delete your data on request, retrieve it for you, or restore it after you remove it — we never have access to it in the first place. Please back up anything you want to keep before uninstalling.

7. Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13 and collects no information from anyone, including children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children because we do not collect personal information at all.

8. Changes to this policy

If the app's functionality changes in a way that affects your privacy — for example, if a future version adds optional features that transmit data — this policy will be updated before that version is released, with a new effective date and version number, and the change will be described here.

Any such feature would be strictly optional and user-initiated. We do not intend to add advertising, analytics, or tracking to this app.
